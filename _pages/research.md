## Working papers

### "Peer effects and achievement gaps in the Peruvian education system" (with Johar Cassa)

In the last decade, important efforts have been made in Peru to expand access to secondary education; however, quality has not kept pace with this expansion and its heterogeneity has worsened, mainly among private schools. Taking educational performance as an indicator of educational quality, the present study has as its first objective to identify the differences in the educational performance of high school students attending public and private schools, and if these differences persist once the composition has been incorporated school, for which, an educational production function is estimated. A second objective is to explain those factors that lead to the gap in educational performance among students in public schools and private schools. The results show that, the differences initially observed in school performance in favor of private schools decrease drastically and cease to be significant once it is controlled by the socioeconomic composition of the schools. This suggests that the type of school management is not clearly associated with differences in student performance in any of the areas evaluated by PISA. Likewise, it was found that the gap in school performance between public and private school students would be explained mainly by school composition and the variables associated with the student and his family.

### "Analysis of urban informal self-employment in Peru during the period 2012 - 2016"

Informal self-employment concentrates the most vulnerable groups of the Peruvian labor market that works in low productivity activities and has limited access to social protection services; however, studies on informal self-employment are almost non-existent. In this context, this research seeks to analyze the heterogeneity of the urban informal self-employment sector in Peru during the 2012-2016 period, for which the data from the ENAHO is used and the methodology of Grimm et al. (2013) based on the estimation of a binomial probit model. The results of the research that showed the sector of urban informal self-employment in Peru is heterogeneous, in addition to being composed of the informal self-employed of the "upper stratum" (6%) and informal self-employed of the "surviving lower stratum" (84%), it includes a third group of informal self-employed from the "lower stratum" potential to belong to the "upper stratum" (10%).
